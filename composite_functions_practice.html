We Do
We found that the domain of \( (g \circ f)(x) = \dfrac{1}{x-2} \) is \( (2, \infty) \).
How does this compare with the domain of \( m(x) = \dfrac{1}{x-2} \) considered as a standalone function?
Standalone m(x) has domain x ≠ 2, i.e. (−∞,2) ∪ (2,∞). The composition restricts to only x > 2.
