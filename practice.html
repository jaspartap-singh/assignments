1 Diagonals of a Parallelogram — Finding an Angle

The diagonals of parallelogram ABCD intersect at O.
Given: ∠BOC = 90° and ∠BDC = 50°.
Find ∠OAB.

∠OAB = degrees
In △BOC: ∠BOC = 90°, ∠BCO = ∠BDC = 50° (alternate angles, BC ∥ AD). So ∠OBC = 40°. In a parallelogram, AB ∥ DC, so ∠OAB = ∠OBC (alternate interior angles with transversal AB).

2 Triangle with Parallel Lines — Perimeter of △PQR

Through vertices A, B, and C of △ABC, lines are drawn parallel to BC, CA, and AB respectively, forming triangle PQR.
The perimeter of △ABC is 27 cm.
What is the perimeter of △PQR?

Perimeter of △PQR = cm
Each side of △ABC is the midsegment of △PQR. A midsegment is half the parallel side, so each side of △PQR = 2 × corresponding side of △ABC. Thus perimeter of △PQR = 2 × perimeter of △ABC.

3 Angle Bisector in a Parallelogram

In parallelogram ABCD, the bisector of ∠A bisects side BC at X.
If AB = 17 units, what is the measure of AD?

AD = units
Since AX bisects ∠A, ∠DAX = ∠BAX. Also ∠BAX = ∠AXB (alternate angles since AD ∥ BC). So △ABX is isosceles with AB = BX. Since X bisects BC, BX = BC/2. In a parallelogram AD = BC. So AD = BC = 2·BX = 2·AB = 2×17 = 34. Wait — re-examine: BX = AB = 17, and AD = BC = 2·BX... Re-check with the isosceles result: AB = BX = 17, and AD = BC = 2×17 = 34? Actually AD ∥ AB... use the isosceles triangle result carefully: AB = BX, and since X is the midpoint of BC, BX = XC = BC/2, so BC = 2AB. Since AD = BC in a parallelogram, AD = 2×17 = 34.

4 Trisection of Diagonal BD — Fractions (7a)

Points P and Q trisect (divide into three equal parts) diagonal BD of parallelogram ABCD. O is the intersection of the diagonals AC and BD.

PO is what fraction of BD?
QO is what fraction of BD?
The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other, so O is the midpoint of BD, meaning BO = OD = BD/2. P trisects BD from B, so BP = BD/3. Therefore PO = BO − BP = BD/2 − BD/3 = BD/6. Similarly, Q is 2/3 from B, so BQ = 2BD/3. QO = BQ − BO = 2BD/3 − BD/2 = BD/6.

6 Rhombus Extension — Is ∠G = 90°? (8a)

In the figure, ABCD is a rhombus. Side AB is extended on both sides to segment EF such that EA = AB = BF. Lines ED and FC produced meet at G.
Is the measure of ∠G equal to 90°?

The measure of ∠G must be 90°. Is this true or false?

In a rhombus all sides are equal. EA = AB = BF. The triangles EAD and FBC are congruent (SAS). You can use angle-chasing: let ∠DAB = 2α. Then ∠EAD = 180°−2α, and in isosceles △EAD, ∠AED = ∠ADE = α. Similarly angles at F. Then in the triangle formed at G, the angles sum to 180°. You'll find ∠G = 90°.
